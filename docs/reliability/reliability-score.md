# CertLayer Reliability Score

The CertLayer Reliability Score is a standardized metric that measures the operational reliability of protocols monitored by CertLayer.

The score provides a transparent and verifiable signal of infrastructure reliability. Instead of relying on reputation or marketing claims, stakeholders can evaluate protocol performance using measurable operational data.

## Purpose

The reliability score helps answer key questions such as:

- How often does a protocol experience downtime?
- How quickly are incidents resolved?
- Does the protocol consistently meet reliability commitments?

This transforms infrastructure performance into an objective reliability signal.

## How the Score Works

Each protocol monitored by CertLayer receives a reliability score derived from operational metrics and incident history.

**Inputs include:**

- Uptime performance
- Number of recorded incidents
- Severity of incidents
- Incident resolution speed
- Successful enforcement of reliability commitments

The score updates dynamically as new operational data becomes available.

## Scoring Model

| Metric | Impact |
|---|---|
| Protocol uptime | Positive |
| Incident frequency | Negative |
| Incident severity | Negative |
| Fast incident resolution | Positive |
| Proven reliability commitments | Positive |

The final score represents an aggregated reliability rating over time.

## Reliability Tiers

| Tier | Score Range | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Platinum | 95–100 | Extremely reliable infrastructure |
| Gold | 85–94 | Highly reliable |
| Silver | 70–84 | Moderate reliability |
| Bronze | Below 70 | Reliability concerns |

These tiers allow users and partners to quickly understand a protocol's reliability profile.

## Public Transparency

Reliability scores are accessible through the CertLayer Reputation Explorer.

The explorer provides:

- Protocol reliability rankings
- Incident history
- Uptime performance metrics
- Enforcement outcomes

This transparency encourages protocols to maintain strong operational standards.

## Incentive Alignment

The reliability scoring system aligns incentives across the ecosystem.

Protocols benefit from strong reliability scores because:

- Users prefer dependable infrastructure
- Partners choose reliable integrations
- Investors value operational transparency

Poor reliability performance becomes visible, creating accountability.

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term vision of the CertLayer Reliability Score is to become the industry standard for measuring Web3 infrastructure reliability.

Just as credit scores measure financial reliability, CertLayer aims to measure protocol reliability across decentralized ecosystems and multiple chains.

## Relationship to Incident Enforcement

The reliability score is directly influenced by CertLayer's incident lifecycle — when incidents occur and are finalized, they contribute to a protocol's reliability record.
